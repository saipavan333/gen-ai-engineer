Track 1 · Visual Lab

Watch a prediction happen

Everything Track 1 explained — tokens, embeddings, attention, probabilities, sampling — is one loop that runs over and over. Here it is, running, with nothing hidden. Press play and watch a single word get chosen.

How to use this lab

Press ▶ Play to watch the full cycle, or Step to move one stage at a time and read what each one does. Change the sentence, change the temperature, and run it again.

Make it yours

1. Set temperature to its lowest and run it five times. Then set it to its highest and run it five times. What changes — the probabilities, or the choice?
2. Type a sentence ending in a word the model has never seen. Watch stage 4. What does it do when it has no good options?
3. Watch stage 3 closely. Which earlier word does the last word lean on most, and does that match how you would read the sentence?

▸ Show reasoning

1. Temperature never changes what the model believes — stage 4 is identical every time. It only reshapes the distribution just before the draw in stage 5. Low temperature keeps picking the favourite; high temperature spreads the chance around. That is the whole difference between "reliable" and "creative", and it costs nothing to change.

2. It still produces a distribution — it always does. With no strong signal the bars flatten out and the choice becomes close to arbitrary. A model has no mechanism for "I don't know"; it only has flatter probabilities. That is precisely why grounding and refusal rules have to be engineered around it.

What you just saw
  • Text becoming tokens — the units the model actually reads.
  • Tokens becoming vectors, so meaning turns into arithmetic.
  • Attention reaching back to decide which earlier words matter.
  • A probability distribution over every possible next token.
  • Sampling — one draw, shaped by temperature.
  • The token appended, and the whole loop running again.

A frontier model differs in scale, and in learning far richer patterns than this one. The loop is the same.
