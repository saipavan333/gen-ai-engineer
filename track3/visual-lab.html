A question's journey through RAG

RAG is usually drawn as boxes and arrows, which hides the one idea that matters: retrieval is geometry. Watch a question become a point in space, find its nearest neighbours, and come back with an answer it can prove.

How to use this lab

Press ▶ Play for the whole journey, or Step to pause on each stage. Switch the question and run it again — watch where it lands, and which documents light up.

Make it yours

1. Run “is postage free”. The word postage never appears in any document — so how does it still find the shipping snippet?
2. Watch stage 4 for each question. What would happen at stage 6 if the two nearest documents were both irrelevant?
3. Only two chunks are kept. What breaks if you keep twenty instead?

▸ Show reasoning

1. Because nothing here matches words. Both the question and the document became positions in a shared space, and “postage” lands near “shipping” because they were used in similar contexts during training. That is the entire reason RAG beats keyword search on natural phrasing — and also why it can miss an exact product code that keyword search would nail.

2. The model would answer anyway, fluently and wrongly, from whatever it was handed. Retrieval failure does not look like an error — it looks like a confident answer. That is why you measure recall separately from answer quality.

3. Cost and latency rise with every token, and accuracy often falls: the relevant chunk gets buried among near-misses, and facts in the middle of a long context are the ones models most often overlook. More retrieved text is not more knowledge.

What you just saw
  • Documents becoming points in a space, not keywords in an index.
  • A question embedded by the same model, into the same space.
  • Retrieval as measuring distance — the whole mechanism, in one step.
  • The context window being assembled, and becoming the only thing the model knows.
  • An answer that can be traced to a source.
