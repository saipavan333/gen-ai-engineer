Watch a document become data

Turning a scanned invoice into database rows is the highest-value multimodal task in business — and the easiest to get dangerously wrong. Watch the read, and then watch the four stages that exist only to stop a confident wrong number reaching your accounts.

How to use this lab

Press ▶ Play for the whole pipeline, or Step through it. The lesson is in the stages after the read: validate, cross-check, and route — accuracy alone doesn't make extraction shippable.

Make it yours

1. Imagine the invoice total read as 1600.99 instead of 160.99. Which stage catches it — and would a vision model reading the document alone have noticed?
2. The date 14/03/2025 is unambiguous, but 03/04/2025 is not. What should the system do when both day-first and month-first parse to valid dates?

▸ Show reasoning

1. The independent cross-check catches it — the line items sum to 160.99 and no longer match the stated total, so it routes to a human. A vision model reading the document alone would report 1600.99 with complete confidence, because it has nothing to compare against. A second, independent calculation beats a better reader.

2. Don't guess. Either apply a locale or vendor convention you can justify and record, or flag it for review. A silently mis-parsed date shifts a payment by months and nothing in the output looks wrong — the most dangerous kind of error is the one that produces a clean, confident, incorrect result.
